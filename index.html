Built for Speed

There's a device called a shutter that sits in front of the digital SLR sensor, blocking it from light. When you take a photo, the shutter (made up of two curtains) opens and closes.

A Lens for Every Occasion

Digital SLR is the perfect camera! If want to take landscape photos, just attach a wide-angle lens. To photograph bugs and flowers, use a macro lens. When taking photos of your pets, just use a telephoto lens and can snap shots even when you are halfway across a field.

Fast Zoom

One camera, tons of lenses.
You manually control the zoom of a digital SLR, and can zoom as fast or slow as you like. That's it!

High Quality in Low Light

One clear advantage of digital SLR cameras is that they're able to capture photos even when there's not a lot of ambient light. Digital SLRs have a feature called ISO, it makes the digital sensor more sensitive to light.
